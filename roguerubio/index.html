Executive Summary

Rubio does not capture objectives. Rubio becomes them. The Firm's most mission-committed infantry specialist operates under a doctrine that is, at first glance, almost disquieting in its simplicity: the objective must be taken, the Rogue will take it. What distinguishes this operative from the broader infantry category is not superior positioning — it is an absolute refusal to accept that failure is a conclusion rather than a temporary condition.

Filed Statement — Matter of the Objective
"He will die on it. He has died on it. He will die on it again. And when he returns — he will return to the objective. This is not recklessness. This is jurisprudence."

Most operatives treat death as a reason to reassess. The Rogue treats it as a minor procedural delay. He respawns, recalculates the fastest route back to the contested zone, and resumes prosecution of the mission with no observable reduction in commitment. For enemies holding an objective, Rubio's presence in the game is not an inconvenience — it is a sustained challenge they cannot afford to ignore.

Areas of Practice
Objective Seizure & Retention
Primary specialty. Rubio's doctrine holds that every map has a decisive point, and his position at that point is non-negotiable. Whether the objective is defended, contested, or under full enemy control — he is already en route.
Attrition by Presence
A continuous commitment to contested space forces enemies to divert resources, abandon rotations, and make reactive rather than proactive decisions. Rubio's mere presence on the objective is a tactical drain on enemy attention.
Spawn-Cycle Optimization
Where others treat respawn as downtime, Rubio treats it as transit. His return-to-objective speed is a measurable combat multiplier — the enemy never fully consolidates because the next engagement begins before the last one ends.
Mission Doctrine Enforcement
Rubio serves as The Firm's moral compass on mission priority. His presence reminds all operatives of what matters: not the kill count, not the streak — the objective. Always the objective.

Closing Argument

The Firm has retained operatives who win gunfights. It has retained operatives who dominate from range. Rubio provides something rarer and, in a broader strategic sense, more valuable: an operative who will not leave the field without completing the mission.

Death, in Rubio's operational calculus, is not a deterrent. It is a temporary change of venue. The objective remains. Rubio returns. The enemy is strongly advised to account for this in their positioning.
